The core distinction is structural. A direct gilt offers defined sterling cash flows at a known maturity, while an ETF offers access to a basket whose behaviour depends on the underlying index, duration profile and trading conditions.

That makes many headline comparisons misleading. A gilt ETF can diversify duration exposure, but it does not reproduce the certainty of holding one bond to redemption. A global equity ETF can improve diversification, but it introduces an entirely different risk and return profile.

For the portfolio, this source note underlines the essay's main point: the analytical error is to compare labels without first clarifying the economic problem the investor is trying to solve.
